Good aesthetics and visuals have always brought joy to me. Whether it's a massive wall art graffiti or just a simple sketch on a piece of paper.

These observations have always, genuinely made me think how could I have done this or how could I have made it better. This flow of thought has always been a integral part of my thinking process when it comes to designing either something from scratch (that is, to see what others are doing, and trying to find it's unique place in the universe) or redesigning something which exists already.

I have worked on website designs, from e-com to highly creative web innovations, starting from designing for desktop browsers to designing apps, from simple neat graphics for social media to understanding the design of the world, from keeping my core focus on digital design to side hustling in print and beyond, from starting as a super curious intern to founding a much loved company with my beloved mates.

After covering most of the skills which I feel a designer in the digital field must learn, my tangent has moved towards conversations with people who want to work with me.

I have started to understand that without truly knowing and understanding the people I work with or rather I work for, I should not move forward with any approach to design, regardless of how skilled or experienced I am.

So, I am looking forward to working with people who are enthusiastic and passionate about what they do and what they want to achieve, so that when we sit together we create something fabulous.
